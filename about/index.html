I'm an astrophysics PhD student at Queen's University Belfast. You can check out my academic profile here.
My main research interest is using large-scale statistical analysis and Monte-carlo modelling to detect and analyse below-noise floor signatures. This has taken the form of Solar as well as Stellar nanoflares, but also involves Biomedical image analysis. Nanoflares are Solar flare events, with a billionth the energy of a traditional solar flare. While less energetic, they are much more frequent, with theoriticaly thousands of nanoflares occuring each second in the sun. Against the bright background of the sun, or a star, these nanoflares lie below the noise floor, leaving them unretreivable by standard analysis. My research has focused on searching for the tiny statistical signatures which distinguish these nanoflares from the background noise. The simple explanation is that standard noise follows a standard distrubution in a large enough sample, so examining the distribution of statitical fluctuation in a seemingly noisy star lightcurve, we can search for any statistical offset which is consistent with our Monte-carlo modelled nanoflare signals. These techniques are explained in more detail in the publications Jess et al (2019) and Dillon et al (in submission)

These statistical and analytical techniques are initially developed with Biomedical applications and then are adpated for use in Astrophysical contexts.
